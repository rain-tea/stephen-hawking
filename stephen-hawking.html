Stephen Hawking (1942â€“2018) was a British theoretical physicist known for his work on black holes and cosmology. Diagnosed with ALS at 21, he defied expectations and continued groundbreaking research.

His book, "A Brief History of Time" (1988) became a global bestseller, explaining complex physics to the public. Hawking proposed that black holes emit radiation (Hawking radiation), revolutionizing our understanding of the universe.

Despite being wheelchair-bound and communicating via a speech-generating device, he remained an active researcher and public figure. He held the Lucasian Professorship at Cambridge, was a fellow of the Royal Society, and received numerous awards, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

His life and work inspired millions, proving that the human mind knows no limits.
